Excursion to Health Domain: Data Management System for Kidney Transplant Unit, NHSL

Feb 16, 2021

In 2013 and 2014, I became an active participant to the discussions on National Foundation for Open-Source Health Software (NFOSHS), and participated Post-Scientific Sessions of Symposium on “eHealth in Sri Lanka: the progress so far and the way forward” held in March, 2013 as a collaboration between College of Medical Administrators of Sri Lanka (MSASL) & Health Informatics Society of Sri Lanka (HISSL). I was able to get the support of many professionals in the medical domain and were able to contribute my IT knowledge with them.

On one occasion, I met Prof. Saroj Jayasinghe, then Head/Dept. of Clinical Medicine, Faculty of Medicine, University of Colombo. He requested me to see the possibility of developing a data management system to support patients who undergo Kidney Transplant and maintain their clinical records to best serve them in post-surgery clinics. At the same time, we started developing a hospital management system with the Dompe hospital, a group of final year students led by Ms. Nalini Galmangoda took the challenge. One of Prof. Jayasinghe’s subordinates, Dr. Rushika Lanerolle worked closely with students to give the requirement of such a system that he used during his overseas training.

To gratefully acknowledge the efforts of these students Prof. Saroj Jayasinghe was very kind to issue the following letter in May, 2014.

Excursion to Health Domain: Investigating eHospital Dompe Project

Feb 4, 2021

Students who worked with me in the Cancer Institute, Maharagama projects wanted to do their final year project on Health Information Systems in 2013. Since groups size was smaller than the 2nd year project groups, only four students were keen to develop a Hospital management Software for government hospitals of Sri Lanka.

Three students, Miyuru Madhawa de Silva, Imanka Kodithuwakku and Rusiru Perera with one new student Vishwa Jayadewa wanted to develop a “SOA Powered Software Framework for a Stable Health Information System”. We decided to work on this topic since we were not satisfied with the systems we had at that era. We came to know that there is a Hospital Health Information Management System developed with the help of ICTA running at Dompe District Hospital and a few other places. At the same time we got to know about OpenMRS. We inquired which one is best suited for Sri Lankan context. This went into a medical forum through Prof. Vajira Dissanayake. One of his students, Dr. Roshan Hewapathirana, who was doing a PhD in Health Informatics in University of Oslo clarified HHIMS is based on the Sri Lankan care pathway and fits into local needs.

Then we thought of studying HHIMS. We inquired about the source code of that software to develop something on top of that, but it was not available to us. We had long email conversations with ICTA and Dr. Denham Pole, who was in the process of developing that software, but unable to locate an architectural design of it and even the code, even though it is described as an Open Source software.

We were introduced to Dr. Sampath Kulathilaka, a leading medical officer who was working at eDompe Hospital project by ICTA warmly welcomed us at eDompe Hospital and explained us the clinical protocols and process flows in Hospital patient management. He extended his fullest support to us with two other gentlemen Mr. Shriyananda Rathnayake and Dr. Indika Jagoda. This trio was in continuous touch to help us to understand the problem domain. There were four student groups lead by Mr. Miyuru Madhawa de Silva (SOA powered software Ms. Umayangani Dahanayake (developing hospital process maps using Bizagi) , Ms. Nalini Galmangoda (developing in ward system for Renal Transplant Unit, NHSL) , Mr. Gihan Kaushalya (studying usability of HHIMS).

Few photos taken at our first visit to eDompe reminds us how we developed our knowledge on Hospital process flows.


Excursion to Health Domain: an HIS at National Cancer Institute

Feb 3, 2021

Working closely with students is beneficial to teachers, students and even to the entire society. Students are young, enthusiastic, and their intentions are pure and humane. Working closely with them helps teachers in many ways. My excursion to health domain started by a group of students who wanted to develop a software for health domain.

In 2011 June, Anupiya Nugaliyadde, Miyuru Madhawa de Silva, Imanka Kodithuwakku and Rusiru Perera were in 2nd year and wanted to do their 2nd year IT project in the health domain. I put them in contact with Consultant Pediatric Oncologist Dr. Wasantha Rathnayaka, introduced to me by one of my friends, Dr. Ganganath Gunathilaka. Students visited National Cancer Institute, Maharagama and started developing a Health Information System to maintain Electronic Medical Records (EMR) of pediatric patients in the Pediatric Ward of the National Cancer Institute. On my supervision, they have completed the small HIS system successfully. Doctors wanted to put that system on Live, but they were short of one computer to connect their PC in the office with the ward. SLIIT donated a used computer to make a small LAN to access patient records from the ward.
